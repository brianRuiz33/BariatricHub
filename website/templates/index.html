GASTRIC SLEEVE

Gastric sleeve surgery is a minimally invasive procedure that reduces the stomach by 75–80%, helping patients feel full sooner and eat less. Most patients lose 60–80% of their excess weight within 12–18 months.
It is performed laparoscopically and often improves conditions such as type 2 diabetes and high blood pressure.


ENDOSCOPIC GASTRIC SLEEVE

Endoscopic gastric sleeve is a non-surgical weight-loss procedure that reduces stomach volume using internal sutures placed through the mouth — without incisions.
It helps patients feel full sooner, control portions, and achieve meaningful weight loss. The procedure is outpatient, minimally invasive, and typically involves a faster recovery compared to traditional bariatric surgery.

GASTRIC BALLOON

The gastric balloon is a non-surgical weight-loss procedure in which a soft, saline-filled balloon is placed in the stomach to help reduce hunger and control portions.
It is ideal for patients who are not ready for surgery or need a less invasive option to begin their weight-loss journey. The procedure is temporary, outpatient, and requires no incisions or permanent changes to the stomach.



ONE ANASTOMOSIS GASTRIC BYPASS

One Anastomosis Gastric Bypass is a minimally invasive procedure that reduces stomach size and reroutes part of the intestine to promote significant weight loss.
It combines restriction and metabolic effects to enhance weight reduction. The surgery is performed laparoscopically and is ideal for patients needing a more powerful solution.

Roux-en-Y gastric bypass

Roux-en-Y gastric bypass is a minimally invasive bariatric procedure that reduces stomach size and reroutes the small intestine to promote substantial weight loss.
It limits food intake while altering digestion to improve metabolism and control hunger. This procedure is highly effective for patients with severe obesity or obesity-related conditions such as type 2 diabetes.

Intestinal Bipartition

Intestinal Bipartition is a minimally invasive metabolic surgery that reroutes part of the small intestine to enhance weight loss and improve blood sugar control. Food travels through two intestinal pathways, increasing hormonal response without removing any portion of the stomach.
It promotes significant, sustainable weight loss while preserving normal digestion.
