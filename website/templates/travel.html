Travel & Safety Guide

For U.S. Patients Traveling to Tijuana for Bariatric Surgery

Airport & Transportation

Patients typically arrive at San Diego International Airport (SAN), located about 30 minutes from the U.S.–Mexico border.

  • Day 1: Airport → Hotel
  • Day 2–3: Hotel ↔ Hospital
  • Day 4: Hospital → Hotel
  • Day 5: Hotel → Airport

Private transportation is coordinated in advance for surgical patients.

Hotel Accommodations

Your package includes 2 hotel nights at a secure hotel near the hospital.

  • Extra nights available at additional cost
  • Subject to availability
  • Must be requested in advance

Recommended Hotels:

  • Quartz Hotel & Spa Tijuana
  • Grand Hotel Tijuana

Hospital Stay & Safety

  • Surgery day
  • 1 additional recovery day

Procedures are performed in modern private hospitals with board-certified surgeons, advanced laparoscopic equipment, and post-operative monitoring.

Required Documentation

Valid U.S. Passport
Tourist Permit (FMM)
Return Flight Confirmation

Important: While documentation requirements may vary in practice when entering Mexico by land, U.S. Customs and Border Protection requires a valid passport or approved travel document for re-entry into the United States.

Return flight confirmation is not required for patients crossing by land.
